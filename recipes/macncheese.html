Mac and cheese

Description

A classic, comforting dish, homemade mac and cheese offers a depth of flavor and creaminess that box mixes can rarely match. It typically starts with a rich, velvety cheese sauce, known as a mornay sauce, made by incorporating shredded cheese into a foundational butter and flour mixture (roux) and milk. Common cheese choices often blend a sharp cheddar for flavor with a creamy cheese like Gruyère or Monterey Jack for texture, creating a complex, well-balanced profile. The addition of seasonings like dry mustard powder, a dash of Worcestershire sauce, or a pinch of smoked paprika helps to elevate the savory notes, making each bite more satisfying than the last.

Once the sauce is perfectly smooth and seasoned to taste, it is combined with cooked elbow macaroni. Many recipes then transfer this mixture to a baking dish for the final, crucial step: baking. A topping of buttery breadcrumbs, crushed crackers, or an extra layer of shredded cheese bakes into a golden-brown, crispy crust that provides a delightful textural contrast to the soft, luscious pasta underneath. The result is a warm, bubbly, and deeply satisfying dish that serves as the ultimate comfort food, perfect for family dinners, potlucks, or a cozy night in

Ingredients

  • elbow macaroni or any other small tubular pasta.
  • unsalted butter.
  • all-purpose flour.
  • whole milk.
  • heavy cream or half-and-half.
  • sharp cheddar cheese, freshly shredded and divided.
  • Gruyère cheese, freshly shredded and divided.(can substitute with more cheddar or fontina)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper.
  • smoked paprika. (optional)
  • panko breadcrumbs.
  • shredded Parmesan cheese.

Cooking instructions

  1. Preheat and Prep: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Cook Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the macaroni for about 1 minute less than the package directions for al dente, as it will continue to cook in the oven. Drain and toss with a small drizzle of olive oil or a tablespoon of butter to prevent sticking.
  3. Make the Roux: In a large saucepan or Dutch oven, melt 5 tablespoons of the butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour, salt, pepper, and paprika. Cook, whisking constantly, for about 1-2 minutes until the mixture is lightly golden and smells slightly toasted.
  4. Add Dairy: Slowly pour in the milk and heavy cream, a little at a time, whisking constantly until the mixture is smooth and lump-free. Continue cooking and whisking over medium heat until the sauce has thickened enough to coat the back of a spoon, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Melt the Cheese: Remove the saucepan from the heat. Gradually stir in 4 cups of the shredded cheddar and all of the Gruyère cheese until completely melted and the sauce is smooth.
  6. Combine and Layer: Add the cooked pasta to the cheese sauce and stir to combine fully. Pour half of the pasta mixture into the prepared baking dish. Sprinkle with the remaining 2 cups of shredded cheddar. Top with the rest of the mac and cheese mixture.
  7. Add Topping and Bake: In a small bowl, combine the panko breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, and the remaining 1 tablespoon of melted butter. Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the top of the mac and cheese.
  8. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 20-30 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and the topping is golden brown. Let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
